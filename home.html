CareToClean

The CareToClean(C2C) or Clean India Mission is a nation-wide campaign in India for the period 2014 to 2019 that aims to clean up the streets, roads, and infrastructure of India's cities, towns, and rural areas. The campaign's objectives include eliminating open defecation through the construction of household-owned and community-owned toilets and establishing an accountable mechanism of monitoring toilet use.

Led by the Government of India, the mission aims to achieve an "open-defecation free" (ODF) India by 2 October 2019, the 150th birth anniversary of Mahatma Gandhi, by constructing 90 million toilets in rural India at an estimated cost of ₹1.96 lakh crore (US$28 billion).

Key Objectives

Eliminate Open Defecation

Our mission is to promote the use of toilets and end open defecation practices across rural and urban areas. We collaborate with local governments to install affordable sanitation facilities, conduct awareness campaigns, and provide education on hygiene. To date, we have supported over 500 communities, impacting more than 250,000 individuals with improved sanitation access.

Solid Waste Management

We implement efficient systems for waste collection, segregation, and disposal to create cleaner environments. Our programs include door-to-door waste pickup, recycling initiatives, and community-led composting. Over the past year, we’ve managed to divert 30% of waste from landfills in partnered regions, benefiting over 100,000 households.

Behavioral Change

We focus on educating and motivating communities to adopt hygienic practices through workshops, school programs, and media campaigns. Our goal is to foster a culture of cleanliness and health awareness. We’ve reached over 50,000 people with training sessions, resulting in a 40% increase in proper waste disposal habits in target areas.

Capacity Building

We strengthen local bodies and organizations to manage sanitation effectively by providing training, resources, and technical support. Our initiatives include leadership development and infrastructure planning. We’ve empowered 200+ local teams, enabling them to sustain sanitation projects independently in their regions.

Water Sanitation

Our water sanitation efforts ensure access to clean and safe water for all communities through water purification systems and regular testing. We’ve installed 150+ filtration units, serving 75,000 people, and conduct monthly water quality checks to maintain standards and prevent disease outbreaks.

Sustainable Infrastructure

We develop eco-friendly sanitation facilities designed for long-term impact, using renewable materials and energy-efficient technologies. Our projects include solar-powered toilets and biodegradable waste systems. So far, we’ve built 80 sustainable facilities, reducing environmental impact by 25% in supported areas.
